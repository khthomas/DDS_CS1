Market for New Craft Brewery Market in the United States.

Prepared by Joe Scheder and Kyle Thomas for New Brewery Inc.

New Brewery Inc. would like to introduce a new IPA with a high alcohol content and bitterness. They would like to introduce this to a large market that currently favors having breweries producing beers with these characteristics.

The primary goal of this analysis was to understand the craft brewery market in the United States. There were additional sub goals including:

  1. Merging two datasets so that linked beer production with brewery location to find the median International Bitterness Unit (IBU) content of beers on a state by state basis. Find the median Alocohol Content By Volume (ABV) of beers on a state by state basis.
  2. Generate visualizations so that this data can be quickly and easily understood.
  • Determine which state had the strongest beer (highest IBU)
  • Determine which state had the most bitter beer (highest ABV)
  • Determine if there is a relationship between bitterness and alcohol content.

Based on the analysis of the data for breweries by state, there is reason to believe that states that there are certain states that have both high median alcohol content and bitter beers.

A basic market analysis was conducted to determine which states could use a high ABV and high IBU beer. This was done by first creating a combined metric for IBU and ABV. The new metric was simply multiplying ABV with IBU. Next, the 3 states with the lowest median IBU and ABV beers were identified (the combined metric). The three states with the lowest combined ABV and IBU values are Wisconsin, Kansas, and Wyoming. Interestingly, these states are within a reasonable trucking distance, so this new brewery could be located in a low cost area and the beer could be shipped to these identified states.
